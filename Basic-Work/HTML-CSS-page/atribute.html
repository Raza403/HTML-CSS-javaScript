Abdul Sattar Edhi

The man who made and ran the world's largest volunteer ambulance network.

Abdul Sattar Edhi was a Pakistani philanthropist, ascetic, and humanitarian who founded the Edhi Foundation, which runs the world's largest volunteer ambulance network,[10] along with homeless shelters, rehab centres, and orphanages across Pakistan

Here is timeline of Abdul Sattar Edhi's life

  • Edhi born in 1928
  • Moved to Karachi where he established a free dispensary for Karachi's low-income residents.
  • Edhi's charitable activities expanded in 1957 when an Asian flu epidemic swept through Karachi.
  • Donations allowed him to buy his first ambulance the same year. He later expanded his charity network with the help of his wife Bilquis Edhi.
  • By the time of his death, Edhi was registered as a parent or guardian of nearly 20,000 children.
  • He is known as Angel of Mercy and is considered to be Pakistan's "most respected" and legendary figure.
  • In 2013, The Huffington Post claimed that he might be "the world's greatest living humanitarian"
  • while on 28 February 2017, Google celebrated Edhi with a Google Doodle hailing his "super-efficient" ambulance service.
  • Eidhi received several awards including Gandhi Peace Award and the UNESCO-Madanjeet Singh Prize.
